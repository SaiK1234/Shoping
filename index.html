FACT 1

Water consumption

The fashion industry is the second-largest consumer of water worldwide. It takes about 700 gallons of water to produce one cotton shirt and 2000 gallons for one jeans.

FACT 2

Chemical Pollution

The fashion industry is responsible for 20% of global wastewater. Textile dyeing is the second largest polluter of clean water globally.

FACT 3

Textile Waste

The average consumer throws away 70 pounds (31.75 kg) of clothing per year. Globally, we produce 13 million tons of textile waste each year.

FACT 4

Carbon Footprint

The fashion industry is responsible for 10% of annual global carbon emissions, more than all international flights and maritime shipping combined.
